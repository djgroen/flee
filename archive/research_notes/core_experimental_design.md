# Core Experimental Design: S1/S2 × Aspirations-Capabilities\n\n## Overview\n\nThis document outlines the specific experiments to test dual-process cognition effects on migration decision-making through aspirations-capabilities theory. These experiments will form the core analysis before the South Sudan case study.\n\n## Experimental Framework\n\n### **Core Research Questions**\n1. How do S1 vs S2 cognitive modes affect aspiration formation and capability assessment?\n2. Do S2 agents achieve better aspiration-capability matching than S1 agents?\n3. How does network topology influence the effectiveness of different cognitive modes?\n4. What are the measurable differences in migration outcomes between cognitive modes?\n\n## Experiment 1: The Aspiration-Capability Matching Test\n\n### **Objective**\nTest whether S2 agents better match their aspirations with realistic capabilities compared to S1 agents.\n\n### **Topology**\n```\nStar Network:\n    Origin (conflict zone, 1000 population)\n    ├── Destination_A: Close (50km), Poor conditions, Low requirements\n    ├── Destination_B: Medium (150km), Good conditions, Medium requirements  \n    ├── Destination_C: Far (300km), Excellent conditions, High requirements\n    └── Destination_D: Very Far (500km), Outstanding conditions, Very high requirements\n```\n\n### **Agent Configuration**\n```python\n# S1 Agents (n=200)\ns1_agents = {\n    'cognitive_mode': 'S1_only',\n    'aspiration_formation': 'immediate_stimulus',\n    'capability_assessment': 'simple_heuristic',\n    'information_processing': 'local_only'\n}\n\n# S2 Agents (n=200) \ns2_agents = {\n    'cognitive_mode': 'S2_only',\n    'aspiration_formation': 'comprehensive_analysis',\n    'capability_assessment': 'strategic_planning',\n    'information_processing': 'network_integrated'\n}\n\n# Mixed Population (n=200)\nmixed_agents = {\n    'cognitive_mode': 'dynamic_switching',\n    'switching_threshold': 0.5,\n    'social_connectivity': 'variable'\n}\n```\n\n### **Scenario Parameters**\n- **Conflict Escalation**: Gradual increase from 0.1 to 0.8 over 30 days\n- **Resource Distribution**: Agents start with varied financial/social resources\n- **Information Availability**: Destinations have hidden quality attributes revealed through social networks\n\n### **Predictions**\n1. **S1 agents**: Will attempt destinations beyond their capabilities, leading to higher failure rates\n2. **S2 agents**: Will choose destinations that better match their capabilities, achieving higher success rates\n3. **Mixed agents**: Will show intermediate performance with dynamic adaptation\n\n### **Measurements**\n- **Aspiration-Capability Alignment**: Correlation between intended and achievable destinations\n- **Journey Success Rate**: Percentage reaching intended destination\n- **Settlement Quality**: Final destination quality score\n- **Resource Efficiency**: Resources remaining upon arrival\n\n## Experiment 2: The Information Network Utilization Test\n\n### **Objective**\nTest how S1 vs S2 agents utilize social networks for migration decision-making.\n\n### **Topology**\n```\nTree Network with Information Asymmetry:\n    Origin\n    ├── Hub_A (high connectivity, information rich)\n    │   ├── Camp_A1 (hidden excellent conditions)\n    │   └── Camp_A2 (obvious poor conditions)\n    ├── Hub_B (medium connectivity)\n    │   ├── Camp_B1 (obvious good conditions)\n    │   └── Camp_B2 (hidden poor conditions)\n    └── Direct_Route\n        └── Camp_C (obvious medium conditions)\n```\n\n### **Information Structure**\n- **Public Information**: Basic safety and capacity data\n- **Network Information**: Employment opportunities, services, future prospects\n- **Social Information**: Family reunification possibilities, community support\n\n### **Agent Types**\n```python\n# High Connectivity Agents\nhigh_conn_agents = {\n    'social_connections': 8,\n    'information_access': 'full_network',\n    'trust_network_info': True\n}\n\n# Low Connectivity Agents  \nlow_conn_agents = {\n    'social_connections': 1,\n    'information_access': 'local_only',\n    'trust_network_info': False\n}\n\n# Medium Connectivity Agents\nmed_conn_agents = {\n    'social_connections': 4,\n    'information_access': 'partial_network',\n    'trust_network_info': 'selective'\n}\n```\n\n### **Predictions**\n1. **S2 + High Connectivity**: Will discover and utilize hidden excellent destinations\n2. **S1 + High Connectivity**: Will be overwhelmed by information, make suboptimal choices\n3. **S2 + Low Connectivity**: Will make best use of limited information\n4. **S1 + Low Connectivity**: Will rely on obvious but suboptimal choices\n\n### **Measurements**\n- **Information Discovery Rate**: Speed of finding hidden high-quality destinations\n- **Network Information Utilization**: Use of non-local information in decisions\n- **Destination Quality**: Final settlement quality relative to available options\n- **Information Cascade Effects**: Spread of destination knowledge through network\n\n## Experiment 3: The Multi-Stage Journey Planning Test\n\n### **Objective**\nTest capability assessment and resource planning differences between cognitive modes.\n\n### **Topology**\n```\nLinear Multi-Stage Network:\n    Origin → Transit_1 → Transit_2 → Transit_3 → Final_Destinations\n    \nEach stage requires different resources:\n    Stage 1: Financial resources (transport cost)\n    Stage 2: Social connections (accommodation, guidance)\n    Stage 3: Documentation/skills (border crossing, employment)\n    Stage 4: Long-term integration capabilities\n```\n\n### **Resource Requirements**\n```python\nstage_requirements = {\n    'stage_1': {'financial': 100, 'social': 1, 'human_capital': 0.3},\n    'stage_2': {'financial': 50, 'social': 3, 'human_capital': 0.4},\n    'stage_3': {'financial': 200, 'social': 2, 'human_capital': 0.7},\n    'stage_4': {'financial': 300, 'social': 5, 'human_capital': 0.8}\n}\n```\n\n### **Agent Resource Distributions**\n- **High Resource Agents**: Can complete all stages if planned well\n- **Medium Resource Agents**: Can complete 2-3 stages with good planning\n- **Low Resource Agents**: Can complete 1-2 stages maximum\n\n### **Predictions**\n1. **S2 agents**: Will better plan resource allocation across stages, higher completion rates\n2. **S1 agents**: Will exhaust resources early, get stranded at intermediate stages\n3. **Resource-capability matching**: S2 agents will choose journeys matching their capabilities\n\n### **Measurements**\n- **Journey Completion Rate**: Percentage reaching final intended destination\n- **Stranding Rate**: Percentage stuck at intermediate stages\n- **Resource Efficiency**: Optimal vs actual resource utilization\n- **Planning Accuracy**: Predicted vs actual journey requirements\n\n## Experiment 4: The Dynamic Aspiration Adaptation Test\n\n### **Objective**\nTest how S1 vs S2 agents adapt aspirations when conditions change.\n\n### **Topology**\n```\nGrid Network (4x4) with Dynamic Conditions:\n    Multiple destinations with changing attributes:\n    - Camp capacities that fill up over time\n    - New opportunities that emerge\n    - Deteriorating conditions in some locations\n    - Policy changes affecting accessibility\n```\n\n### **Dynamic Events Timeline**\n```python\nevents = {\n    'day_10': 'Popular camp reaches capacity',\n    'day_20': 'New employment opportunities in remote camp',\n    'day_30': 'Border closure affects one route',\n    'day_40': 'Family reunification program announced',\n    'day_50': 'Conflict spreads to previously safe area'\n}\n```\n\n### **Information Propagation**\n- **Immediate**: Local events known instantly\n- **Network**: Information spreads through social connections with delay\n- **Official**: Policy changes announced through formal channels\n\n### **Predictions**\n1. **S2 agents**: Will adapt aspirations based on new information, change plans strategically\n2. **S1 agents**: Will stick to initial aspirations, miss new opportunities\n3. **Network effects**: Well-connected S2 agents will adapt fastest\n\n### **Measurements**\n- **Aspiration Volatility**: Frequency of destination preference changes\n- **Adaptation Speed**: Time to respond to new information\n- **Opportunity Capture**: Success in accessing newly available options\n- **Plan Flexibility**: Ability to adjust migration strategy\n\n## Comparative Analysis Framework\n\n### **Cross-Experiment Metrics**\n1. **Cognitive Mode Effectiveness**: S1 vs S2 performance across all experiments\n2. **Topology Sensitivity**: How network structure affects cognitive mode advantages\n3. **Resource Interaction**: How initial capabilities interact with cognitive processing\n4. **Information Value**: Quantifying the benefit of network information access\n\n### **Statistical Analysis Plan**\n```python\nanalysis_framework = {\n    'primary_comparisons': [\n        'S1_only vs S2_only',\n        'Dynamic vs Fixed cognitive modes',\n        'High vs Low connectivity within cognitive modes'\n    ],\n    'statistical_tests': [\n        'Mann-Whitney U tests for outcome differences',\n        'Chi-square tests for categorical outcomes',\n        'Regression analysis for continuous predictors',\n        'Survival analysis for journey completion'\n    ],\n    'effect_size_measures': [\n        'Cohen\\'s d for continuous outcomes',\n        'Odds ratios for binary outcomes',\n        'Eta-squared for categorical predictors'\n    ]\n}\n```\n\n### **Validation Criteria**\n- **Effect Size**: Minimum Cohen's d = 0.3 (medium effect)\n- **Statistical Significance**: p < 0.05 with Bonferroni correction\n- **Consistency**: Effects replicated across multiple runs\n- **Theoretical Alignment**: Results consistent with dual-process and migration theory\n\n## Implementation Timeline\n\n### **Week 1-2: Core Implementation**\n- Implement aspiration-capability framework in Flee\n- Create S1 vs S2
