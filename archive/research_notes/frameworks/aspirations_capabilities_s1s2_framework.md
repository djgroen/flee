# Dual-Process Cognition × Aspirations-Capabilities Migration Theory Framework\n\n## Theoretical Integration\n\n### **Migration Theory: Aspirations and Capabilities**\n- **Aspirations**: Desire to migrate based on perceived opportunities and life improvements\n- **Capabilities**: Actual ability to migrate (resources, social networks, knowledge, legal status)\n- **Migration occurs when**: Aspirations > Threshold AND Capabilities > Threshold\n\n### **Dual-Process Integration**\n- **System 1 (S1)**: Fast, heuristic assessment of aspirations and capabilities\n- **System 2 (S2)**: Deliberate, analytical evaluation of migration opportunities\n\n### **Key Integration Hypothesis**\n**S1 vs S2 agents will differ in how they assess and act on aspirations and capabilities, leading to different migration patterns and outcomes.**\n\n## Core Theoretical Framework\n\n### **Aspirations Assessment**\n- **S1 Aspirations**: Based on immediate local conditions, simple comparisons\n  - \"This place is dangerous\" → High aspiration to leave\n  - \"I heard that camp has food\" → Aspiration to go there\n- **S2 Aspirations**: Based on comprehensive information, future planning\n  - Considers multiple destinations, long-term prospects\n  - Weighs trade-offs between safety, opportunity, family reunification\n\n### **Capabilities Assessment**\n- **S1 Capabilities**: Immediate resources and obvious constraints\n  - \"I can walk there\" vs \"Too far to walk\"\n  - \"I have money for transport\" vs \"No money\"\n- **S2 Capabilities**: Strategic resource planning and network utilization\n  - Plans multi-stage journeys, resource conservation\n  - Leverages social networks for information and assistance\n\n### **Decision Integration**\n```\nS1 Decision: IF (immediate_danger > threshold) AND (can_move_now) THEN move_to_nearest_safe_place\nS2 Decision: IF (expected_utility_destination > expected_utility_current + moving_costs) THEN move_to_optimal_destination\n```\n\n## Targeted Hypotheses\n\n### **H1: Aspiration Formation Differences**\n\n**H1.1 Information Processing for Aspirations**\n- **Hypothesis**: S2 agents will form more accurate aspirations based on comprehensive information; S1 agents will have aspirations driven by immediate stimuli\n- **Prediction**: S2 agents will have more realistic expectations about destinations; S1 agents will be more influenced by rumors and immediate conditions\n\n**H1.2 Aspiration Stability**\n- **Hypothesis**: S1 aspirations will be more volatile and change with immediate conditions; S2 aspirations will be more stable and goal-oriented\n- **Prediction**: S1 agents will change destination preferences frequently; S2 agents will maintain consistent migration goals\n\n### **H2: Capability Assessment Differences**\n\n**H2.1 Resource Planning**\n- **Hypothesis**: S2 agents will better assess and plan for migration costs; S1 agents will underestimate requirements\n- **Prediction**: S2 agents will have higher success rates in reaching intended destinations; S1 agents will get stranded or settle suboptimally\n\n**H2.2 Social Network Utilization**\n- **Hypothesis**: S2 agents will more effectively leverage social networks to enhance capabilities; S1 agents will rely on individual resources\n- **Prediction**: S2 agents with high social connectivity will outperform S1 agents even with similar individual resources\n\n### **H3: Migration Timing and Patterns**\n\n**H3.1 Preemptive vs Reactive Migration**\n- **Hypothesis**: S2 agents will migrate preemptively based on anticipated future conditions; S1 agents will migrate reactively to immediate threats\n- **Prediction**: S2 agents will leave earlier in conflict escalation; S1 agents will wait until conditions become unbearable\n\n**H3.2 Destination Choice Quality**\n- **Hypothesis**: S2 agents will choose destinations that better match their long-term aspirations; S1 agents will choose based on immediate accessibility\n- **Prediction**: S2 agents will end up in destinations with better long-term outcomes (safety, opportunity, family reunification)\n\n### **H4: Topology and Network Effects**\n\n**H4.1 Information Network Utilization**\n- **Hypothesis**: In well-connected networks, S2 agents will better utilize information to align aspirations with realistic capabilities\n- **Prediction**: S2 advantage will increase with network connectivity; isolated agents will show minimal S1/S2 differences\n\n**H4.2 Hub vs Peripheral Strategies**\n- **Hypothesis**: S2 agents will strategically use hub locations to gather information and resources; S1 agents will avoid hubs due to congestion\n- **Prediction**: S2 agents will spend more time at hub locations and achieve better final outcomes\n\n##
