# 🧠 Cognitive Decision-Making Modes in Displacement

## 📚 **Theoretical Foundation**

The framework models three distinct cognitive decision-making modes based on dual-process theory from cognitive psychology:

## 🚀 **System 1: Fast/Intuitive Decision-Making**

### **What It Represents**
- **Automatic processing**: Quick, emotion-driven responses to threats
- **Heuristic-based**: Uses mental shortcuts and past experience
- **Low cognitive load**: Minimal deliberation or analysis
- **Immediate action**: Fast response to perceived danger

### **In Displacement Context**
- **Quick evacuation decisions** based on immediate threat perception
- **Follows crowd behavior** and social cues
- **Chooses nearest/most obvious routes** to safety
- **May result in inefficient distribution** due to lack of planning

### **Behavioral Characteristics**
- High speed, moderate accuracy
- Reactive rather than proactive
- Emotion-driven decision making
- Limited consideration of alternatives

## 🎯 **System 2: Slow/Analytical Decision-Making**

### **What It Represents**
- **Deliberate processing**: Careful evaluation of options and consequences
- **Rational analysis**: Weighs costs, benefits, and probabilities
- **High cognitive load**: Requires time and mental resources
- **Strategic planning**: Considers multiple factors and outcomes

### **In Displacement Context**
- **Planned evacuation** with careful route selection
- **Considers capacity constraints** and resource availability
- **Optimizes for efficiency** and long-term outcomes
- **May be slower to respond** but achieves better distribution

### **Behavioral Characteristics**
- Lower speed, higher accuracy
- Proactive and strategic
- Logic-driven decision making
- Comprehensive consideration of alternatives

## ⚖️ **Dual Process: Adaptive Decision-Making**

### **What It Represents**
- **Context-dependent switching** between System 1 and System 2
- **Adaptive processing**: Uses fast thinking when needed, slow thinking when possible
- **Balanced approach**: Combines speed with accuracy based on situation
- **Dynamic optimization**: Adjusts strategy based on available information and time

### **In Displacement Context**
- **Initial quick response** (System 1) followed by **strategic adjustment** (System 2)
- **Uses multiple routes** and strategies simultaneously
- **Adapts to changing conditions** and new information
- **Balances speed and efficiency** for optimal outcomes

### **Behavioral Characteristics**
- Moderate speed, high overall effectiveness
- Both reactive and proactive elements
- Context-sensitive decision making
- Flexible strategy adaptation

## 📊 **Research Implications**

### **Individual Level**
- **Personality factors**: Some people naturally favor System 1 vs System 2
- **Stress response**: High stress may push people toward System 1
- **Experience effects**: Prior displacement experience may enhance System 2

### **Population Level**
- **Information availability**: Better information enables System 2 processing
- **Social networks**: Strong networks support both systems differently
- **Cultural factors**: Different cultures may favor different cognitive modes

### **Policy Level**
- **Early warning systems**: Enable System 2 processing through advance notice
- **Infrastructure design**: Should accommodate both fast and planned evacuation
- **Information campaigns**: Can shift population toward more effective cognitive modes

## 🚀 **Bottom Line**

The three cognitive modes represent **different ways people actually think and decide** during crises:

- **System 1**: How people react under stress (fast, emotional, heuristic)
- **System 2**: How people plan when they have time (slow, rational, analytical)  
- **Dual Process**: How people actually behave in real situations (adaptive, context-dependent)

**This isn't just theoretical** - it's based on decades of cognitive psychology research and provides a realistic framework for modeling human decision-making in displacement scenarios.
